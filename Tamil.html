Party Plan

Games

👑 1. "How Well Do You Know the Birthday Star?" (Pub-Style Trivia)

When to play: During dinner (8:15 PM) to keep guests entertained while eating.

How it works: Give each table a mini whiteboard or an answer sheet. The MC asks 8–10 funny, surprising, or nostalgic questions about the celebrant’s life.

Sample Questions:

  • "What was [Name]’s very first car?" (A classic Proton Saga or Perodua Kancil is usually a hilarious local giveaway.)
  • "Which artist's poster did they have on their bedroom wall in college?"
  • "What is their absolute favorite supper spot or late-night order?"
  • "Who broke their arm climbing a tree?"
  • "Who first met the birthday person at university?"
  • "Which cousin lives overseas?"

Why it works: It honors the birthday star while sparking fun debates and storytelling across the tables.

🎲 2. The "Ultimate Intro" Retro Music Trivia

When to play: Right after dinner (8:30 PM), before the cake cutting. It re-energises the room while guests are still seated.

How it works: The DJ plays just the first 2 to 3 seconds of an iconic intro beat (no lyrics). Tables compete against each other to shout out or write down the song title.

The Twist: To cater to this specific crowd, divide the rounds by eras:

  • Round 1: 80s Superstar Rajinikanth & Ilaiyaraaja hits (for the elders).
  • Round 2: 90s AR Rahman & Deva Gaana anthems (for the 40–50 age bracket).
  • Round 3: Early 2000s Malaysian Indian hip-hop classics like Yogi B or Psychomantra.

Why it works: It builds massive nostalgia and gets everyone singing along the moment the DJ drops the answer.

🎶 3. Finish the Song 🎵 (Highly Recommended)

Play 5–10 seconds of a popular Tamil, Hindi, Malay, or English song.

Players must:

  • Continue singing the next line.
  • Name the artist or movie for bonus points.

Include songs like:

  • Old Tamil classics
  • SPB hits
  • A.R. Rahman
  • 90s Bollywood
  • Boney M
  • ABBA
  • Bee Gees
  • Malaysian favourites

🕺 4. Musical Freeze Dance

Everyone dances.

When the music stops:

  • Everyone freezes.
  • Anyone moving is out.
  • Last person wins.

Works well after dinner.

💑 5. Couple Challenge

Invite 4–5 couples.

Ask:

  • Who said "I love you" first?
  • Favourite restaurant?
  • Who is messier?
  • Who apologises first?

Award points for matching answers.

Lots of laughs.

🎈 6. Balloon Pop Challenge

Inside balloons place small challenges.

Examples:

  • Dance for 20 seconds.
  • Tell a joke.
  • Sing an old Tamil song.
  • Moonwalk.

🎬 7. Bollywood vs Kollywood Dance-Off

Create two teams.

Play 30-second songs:

  • Bollywood
  • Kollywood

Crowd chooses the winner.

🎤 8. Lip Sync Battle

Contestants choose a famous song.

Props:

  • Sunglasses
  • Scarves
  • Hats

Judged on performance—not singing.

🎯 9. Lucky Chair

Everyone sits.

Every round remove one chair while music plays.

Unlike musical chairs, nobody is pushed—just keep it light and friendly.

🎲 10. Dice of Dares

Roll a giant dice.

Each number has a challenge:

  • Sing.
  • Dance.
  • Joke.
  • Selfie with strangers.
  • High-five 10 people.
  • Birthday message.

💃 11. The Malaysian "Teh Tarik" Dance-Off

When to play: The dance floor opener (9:10 PM). Excellent for breaking the ice.

How it works: Call up 5 to 6 volunteers (mix up the age groups—uncles vs. nephews works best). The DJ plays a fast-paced track like Appadi Podu or a local street remix.

The Twist: Instead of normal dancing, contestants must bust out dance moves that mimic everyday Malaysian Indian life. The MC shouts out cues every 20 seconds:

  • "Now pull the Teh Tarik!"
  • "Now flip the Roti Canai!"
  • "Now you are late for the KTM train!"
  • "Now do the Rajinikanth sunglass flip!"

Why it works: It is visually hilarious, culturally relatable, and instantly dissolves any dance floor shyness.

📱 12. "Pass the Astro Remote" (The Milestone Edition)

When to play: Right before opening the dance floor to get people moving.

How it works: A giant Indian wedding/party variation of Hot Potato. Instead of a potato, use a prop (like an old TV remote or a decorated coconut). While the DJ plays high-energy music, guests pass it around. When the music stops, whoever is holding it draws a card.

The Tasks (Keep them light and funny):

  • “Recreate the iconic Amitabh Bachchan dance step from Sholay.”
  • “Give the birthday star your best 10-second blessing in formal Tamil.”
  • “Find someone wearing gold on your table and bring them to the stage.”
  • “Tell your spouse one thing you love about them.”
  • “Dance like Rajinikanth.”
  • “Do your best Vijay dance.”
  • “Sing one line from your favourite song.”
  • “Tell your funniest childhood memory.”
